Visual Learning Assistant: Instantly visualizing spoken words (e.g., "Apple", "Dog") into clear videos for special education.

Abstract

The rapid advancement of generative artificial intelligence offers a unique opportunity to bridge the communication and learning gap for children with hearing impairments. This project presents a novel system specifically designed to aid in fundamental vocabulary acquisition—specifically, the visual learning of everyday objects.

Our system acts as a "visual dictionary", allowing users to speak or type a word (in English or Kannada) and instantly watch a generated, accurate video of that object in action. By bypassing traditional static flashcards and utilizing immersive video generation, the framework seeks to significantly reinforce object recognition and accelerate language retention.

Architecture & Pipeline

The system utilizes a multi-stage pipeline to ensure high-quality visual output from simple spoken words.

Input

Accepts Voice/Text in English or Kannada (e.g., "ನಾಯಿ").

Translation

Translates regional language to English (e.g., "Dog").

Prompting

LLM expands "Dog" to "A cinematic video of a cute dog running."

Generation

Latent Diffusion Model synthesizes the final video.
